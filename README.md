# Harris Trading and Exchanges: Market Quality Lab

This project is a practical implementation of key ideas from Larry Harris' *Trading and Exchanges*.

The goal is to study market dynamics not only through price charts, but through the market mechanism that determines real execution quality.

The project focuses on:

- liquidity;
- volatility;
- transaction costs;
- spread and slippage;
- market order vs limit order execution;
- the difference between theoretical and executable trading returns.

We use OHLCV market data to calculate proxy measures of market quality and demonstrate how trading costs affect the performance of a simple strategy.

The main research question is:

> How do liquidity, volatility and transaction costs change the real result of a trading strategy?
